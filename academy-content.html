Module 1: Introduction to the UN and MUN

Welcome to GOMUN. Your journey to becoming a global diplomat starts here.

Lesson 1: Understanding the United Nations

The UN was founded in 1945 to maintain peace and advance human rights. It has 193 member states.

UNGA: Policy-making body. Each country has 1 vote. Resolutions are non-binding.
UNSC: Peace and Security. 15 members. Resolutions are binding.

Lesson 2: Your Role as a Delegate

You represent a country's policy, not your own opinion. You must research your country’s allies, enemies, and past actions.

Module 1 Exercise: Draft a 3-sentence opening statement for your country on the topic of "Climate Change."

Module 2: Rules of Procedure (RoP)

RoP ensures debate stays orderly and fair.

Lesson 1: Motions

Common motions used to move the debate forward:

  • Motion for Moderated Caucus: Formal speeches on sub-topics.
  • Motion for Unmoderated Caucus: Informal lobbying and drafting.

Lesson 2: Points

Use these to communicate with the Chair:

Point of Order: To correct a procedural error.
Point of Inquiry: To ask a question about the rules.

Module 3: Public Speaking & Diplomacy

Lesson 1: Speech Structure

A good speech follows: Hook (Attention), Point (Evidence), and Call to Action (Solution).

Lesson 2: Diplomatic Language

Never use "I think." Instead, use "The delegation of [Country] believes..." or "We urge the committee to..."

Module 4: Research & Position Papers

The Position Paper is your roadmap for the conference.

  • Section 1: Background of the topic.
  • Section 2: Your country's past actions.
  • Section 3: Proposed solutions.
Tip: Use official UN sources (UN.org) and Reuters/BBC for credible data.

Module 5: Resolution Writing

This is the final document the committee votes on.

Operative Clauses

These clauses state exactly what action the UN should take. They start with an action verb like: Urges, Encourages, or Condemns.

Module 6: Negotiation & Lobbying

You cannot pass a resolution alone. You must build a **Bloc** (a group of allies).

Negotiation Tips

  • Find countries with similar problems (e.g., Small Island States on Climate Change).
  • Be willing to compromise on small points to win the big vote.
